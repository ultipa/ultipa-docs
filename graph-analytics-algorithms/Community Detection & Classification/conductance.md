## Overview

Conductance is a natural and widely-adopted notion of community goodness and has the ability to detect both non-overlapping and highly overlapping communities for weighted networks. It is particularly useful when studying random walks in graphs.
- <a href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1004.3539" target="blank">Empirical Comparison of Algorithms for Network Community Detection</a> (2010)
- <a href="https://cs.stanford.edu/~jure/pubs/comscore-icdm12.pdf" target="blank">Defining and Evaluating Network Communities based on Ground-truth</a> (2012)

## Concepts
### Cut
In graph theory, a cut is the partition that divides a graph into two disjoint subsets. The weight of a cut is the sum of weights of the edges crossing the cut.
<center><img width=350 src="https://img.ultipa.cn/img/2024-07-24-15-21-08-cut.png"></center>
The example graph is separated into two clusters, with node A, B and C as Cluster Yellow and node D, E, F,and G as Cluster Blue. The cut here crosses edge AE and edge CD, and the weight of the cut is 2.

### Conductance
Conductance is a metric to measure the quality of a partition. Given a graph <i>G = (V,E)</i>, when it is partitioned into two sets, <i>S</i> and <i>V\S</i>, the conductance is defined as 
<center><img width=350 src="https://img.ultipa.cn/img/2024-07-24-15-22-43-conductance-formula.png"></center>
where <i>δ(S)</i> is defined to be the set of edges of G with exactly one endpoint in set S.
<center><img width=350 src="https://img.ultipa.cn/img/2024-07-24-15-36-35-conductance-pic.png"></center>
The lower the conductance, the better the identified community is. As the example graph above, Cut<sub>2</sub> is better than Cut<sub>1</sub>.
